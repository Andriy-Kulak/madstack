# Meta Ads Creative Analysis Framework

Use this reference when adjusting prompts or reviewing report quality.

## Awareness Stages

- **Unaware**: The ad leads with a broad desire, identity, life moment, or entertainment hook before naming the problem.
- **Problem-aware**: The ad agitates a pain, frustration, symptom, or costly status quo.
- **Solution-aware**: The ad explains the category or mechanism that solves the problem.
- **Product-aware**: The ad compares, demos, or differentiates the specific product.
- **Most-aware**: The ad pushes offer, urgency, install, price, promo, or direct conversion.

## Angle Families

- Pain agitation: dramatizes the current problem.
- Outcome transformation: shows the desired future state.
- Feature demo: shows how the product works.
- Mechanism education: explains why the approach works.
- Social proof: uses testimonials, ratings, reviews, press, or community cues.
- Objection handling: answers skepticism, friction, price, setup, trust, or habit concerns.
- Comparison: contrasts against alternatives or the old way.
- Identity/lifestyle: frames the product as part of who the buyer is or wants to become.
- Offer/direct response: pushes install, trial, discount, bundle, or limited-time action.
- Founder/expert authority: borrows credibility from an expert, founder, clinician, creator, or specialist.

## Report Quality Bar

The report should help a marketer and another LLM create better ads for a related brand. Include:

- Reusable hooks and script patterns that are grounded in observed ads.
- Claims and proof points separated from interpretation.
- Repeated patterns vs one-off tests.
- What the brand seems to believe about the buyer's problem.
- Which funnel stages are overused or underused.
- Creative gaps and opportunities.
- Concrete notes about visuals, pacing, audio, captions, CTA, and landing destination.
